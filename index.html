DarkSIde

Detect, understand, and target dark patterns

Each year, the number of devices and applications we use increases. As we use more technology, the technology also uses us to consume and commodify user data. With DarkSIde, users will learn to identify and resist the design tricks that applications use to distract and deceive. DarkSIde, developed in React Native, uses interactivity to educate mobile app users on the most prevalent design dark patterns we encounter in our digital daily lives. Users can learn, on the go, how to spot dark patterns and then put their knowledge to the test in a quiz.

Future releases will allow users to track and contribute their own knowledge by submiting their own evidence of dark patterns they find out in the wild.

Check out the product demo video and source code!

  • Date: December 2020
  • Course: SI 669: Developing Mobile Experiences
  • Category: UX Engineering, Mobile Development
  • Technologies: React Native, Firebase, Expo, Git

Checklisting API Integration

Full-stack web development for an network software installation tool

Network engineers use an internal Flask web application to install software releases across networks on Akamai, but this was originally a process that required manual intervention to check and monitor Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) of networks. Manual intervention cost time and introduced the possibility of manual errors during the install process, which led to severe incidents in the past. By integrating an internal KPI checks API into the Flask application, my colleagues and I were able to automate the tracking of KPIs for monitoring networks and alerting to degradation of health during the software install process.

  • Client: Akamai Technologies
  • Category: Full-Stack Web Design, Technical Program Management
  • Technologies: JavaScript, Flask, Handlebars templating, APIs, Perforce

Lamplight

Prototype of a tool and resource for asylum seekers in the U.S.

When asylum seekers come to the United States from another country, they are often fleeing difficult and/or dangerous circumstances and leaving their homes, lives, and cultures behind with little more than the clothes on their backs. Finding resources to meet their human needs as they navigate the asylum process in a new country can be challenging and overwhelming, especially if the methods to access information about those resources are not in their native language. Human Rights First requested a tool to help all asylum seekers find reputable resources such as healthcare, food, and shelter through a responsive web application designed to work in several languages.

Plantr

A UX-research based product pitch for a new online community

The last decade has seen record growth in technology and in online communities. However, increased global connectedness has not yielded increased cultural understanding. Instead, today’s online communities include and increase political extremism, acts of violence, nationalism, and increased barriers between communities. Social media has polarized people both nationally and internationally, leading to our inability to connect with others of differing backgrounds, communities, nationalities, and cultures.

Plantr envisions a way for people around the world to intentionally share their life experiences and identity in an attempt to bridge the divide we see in communities both physical and online. It will offer an online community garden that allows users to connect by sharing experiences of community identity, cultural understanding, and mutual empathy with people of different backgrounds than theirs.

Through Plantr, users will be introduced via small, virtual community gardens where they are able to interact with people from various walks of life. They’ll be encouraged through gamification to contribute to and support their Plantr community by completing routine prompts and gardening activities. These activities determine the success and health of their garden and also give users currency they can use to tend to and improve their garden.

Plantr provides a fun and engaging space for users to connect with each other and establish a sense of shared belonging. Plantr will be a product that gives an authentic voice to all people, but especially to those whose stories are rarely heard, and provides a forum for people to intentionally listen and learn. The hope of a community like Plantr is to decrease the rates of depression, anxiety, isolation, and division so prevalent in online communities.

View the product pitch slides and recording, as well as the full research paper.

  • Date: January - April 2021
  • Course: SI 529 Winter 2021: Online Communities
  • Category: UX Research, UX Design, Prototyping

Privacy Impact Assessment of Bumble

An examination of privacy concerns on a popular dating app

Bumble is a dating application that prides itself on putting the safety and security of women first. These values are reflected in some of Bumble’s features. The most notable design feature is Bumble’s matching policy that, in heterosexual matches, requires women to make the first move and initiate the conversation. Additionally, Bumble has implemented features such as profile verification, using machine learning to detect inappropriate photos, and offering paid features that enhance privacy.

While privacy by design is incorporated into some aspects of Bumble, the increasing popularity of dating applications and the increasing amount of online harassment suggest that Bumble would benefit from reviewing its privacy harms and addressing the risk of these harms by introducing additional mitigation measures and design solutions.

My peers and I used a Privacy Impact Assessment, to accomplish the following:
- Present a threshold analysis to justify the use of conducting a PIA on Bumble
- Identify the main stakeholders involved in the Bumble app (Bumble Users, Bumble Inc., and Third-Party Providers) and outlined their vested interest in privacy
- Identify the flow of information through the Bumble app, including points where user information is collected, stored, protected, and shared
- Conduct a privacy risk analysis to identify privacy harms on Bumble, classify their level of risk, and address the extent to which Bumble is compliant with modern privacy legislation
- Identify mitigation measures for each privacy risk and prioritized these mitigations in the form of recommended actions
- Propose mockups of design solutions to two of the identified privacy risks

View the presentation slide deck and recording, as well as the full privacy impact assessment report.

Though we did not collaborate with Bumble for this assessment, we hope they will use a similar process of their own to address the privacy concerns associated with the application in order to improve trust and protect the safety of its consumers. This assessment was a useful exercise in understanding how to gauge privacy risks and mitigations in future software I contribute to.

  • Date: January - April 2021
  • Course: SI 540 Winter 2021: Privacy in Information Technology
  • Category: UX Research, UX Design, Information Flows, Mockups

Facebook Interest Categories

A website exploring the accuracy of the data that Facebook collects about a user's profile

Facebook, like many social networking platforms, collects and shares large amounts of personal data with third parties, such as data brokers and third party advertisers, based on the content that its algorithms perceive as interesting or relevant to individual users. However, Facebook’s privacy settings are notoriously difficult to notice and navigate. Most users rarely investigate what assumptions that Facebook is making about them based on their online engagement. My overall goal was to illustrate the detailed and yet inaccurate nature of targeted advertising. I aimed to create something artistic to serve as visual commentary about the assumptions drawn from data collection and the privacy of a user's data on social media platforms.

Using SnapSVG.js, I gained more experience with JavaScript vector-based drawing libraries.

View the live website, the source code, and the presentation slide deck.
