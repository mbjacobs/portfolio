About

Thanks for visiting. If you’re looking for an academic and/or professional summary of my work, please visit my Resume or LinkedIn. Here’s a more holistic summary of who I am…

I'm a native Oregonian who can't help but smile at the smell of rain on wet pavement. (Apparently there's actually a word for this: petrichor.) After living in Oregon for over 22 years, I am exploring Michigan for the next couple.

I am also curious, nerdy, persistent, and short (5'2"). According to my friends, what I lack in size, I make up for in spirit and sarcasm. (:

From the fall 2019 to the spring of 2021, I am attending the University of Michigan in Ann Arbor, MI on merit scholarship to earn my M.S. degree from the School of Information (UMSI). My focus is on UX/UI research, design, and development.

Prior to attending grad school, I graduated summa cum laude with a B.S. in Computer Science from Pacific University in Forest Grove, OR, a small liberal arts university in the suburbs of Portland.

As a student of computer science, psychology, and philosophy, I often find myself  thinking about problems involving human-computer interaction (HCI) and human-robot interaction (HRI). I am passionate about researching and designing technology that helps others acquire and retain autonomy. I also believe that the interactive technology we create must recognize the humanity of its users and provide accessible interfaces for them to use. I am interested in how we can use computers and robots--embodied computers--to satisfy these needs.

The things I mentioned above are especially critical in developing systems and software that are inclusive of users who are underrepresented in the technology industry, such as the elderly, women/girls, and racial/ethnic minorities. I believe strongly in using the resources I have, in whatever role I am in, to do whatever I can to empower women, and other minorities, in computer science. 

I intend to pursue UX/UI design and development in the Pacific Northwest’s “Silicon Forest,” where I hope to contribute to designing inclusive, intuitive, and accessible technology

Besides being a student, I am a lover of erasable pens, cats, trivia, coloring books, and chai tea lattes. I also love reading and writing. When I am not at work programming, I'm likely curled up in a chair with a book or with pen and paper of my own.

DarkSIde

Detect, understand, and target dark patterns.

Each year, the number of devices and applications we use increases. As we use more technology, the technology also uses us to consume and commodify user data. With DarkSIde, users will learn to identify and resist the design tricks that applications use to distract and deceive. DarkSIde, developed in React Native, uses interactivity to educate mobile app users on the most prevalent design dark patterns we encounter in our digital daily lives. Users can learn, on the go, how to spot dark patterns and then put their knowledge to the test in a quiz.

Future releases will allow users to track and contribute their own knowledge by submiting their own evidence of dark patterns they find out in the wild.
